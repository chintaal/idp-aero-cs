# Glossary

## Active Airbrake

A controllable aerodynamic device that increases drag during flight when deployed.

## Apogee

The maximum altitude reached by the rocket during a flight.

## Coast Phase

The flight phase after motor burnout and before apogee, during which the rocket continues upward due to momentum.

## Drag Coefficient

A dimensionless quantity that relates aerodynamic drag force to dynamic pressure and reference area.

## EKF

Extended Kalman Filter, a nonlinear state-estimation method that linearizes dynamics around the current estimate.

## Edge AI

Running AI or ML inference on local embedded hardware rather than on external computing infrastructure.

## Flight State

The set of variables describing the rocket at a given instant, such as altitude, velocity, acceleration, orientation, and actuator status.

## GNC

Guidance, Navigation, and Control.

## HIL

Hardware-in-the-loop testing, where real hardware interacts with simulated system dynamics in real time.

## IMU

Inertial Measurement Unit, typically containing accelerometers and gyroscopes.

## Model Predictive Control

A control method that optimizes future control actions over a finite prediction horizon.

## PINN

Physics-Informed Neural Network, a neural network trained with both data and physical constraints.

## RocketPy

A rocket trajectory simulation framework that can be used for flight analysis and synthetic dataset generation.

## Surrogate Model

A fast approximation model used in place of a more expensive physical simulation or optimization routine.

## Target Apogee

The desired final altitude that the control system attempts to achieve.
